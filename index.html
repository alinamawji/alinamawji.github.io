Breast Cancer Statistics

Welcome to the interactive slideshow on breast cancer statistics. This presentation will cover various aspects of breast cancer incidence and survival including age, tumor size, race, and marital status.


Important facts:

  • Breast cancer is the most common cancer among women worldwide
  • Certain factors can increase risk, such as age, family history, and inherited gene mutations
  • Detecting breast cancer early through regular screenings significantly improves survival rates

For more details, check out the dataset on Kaggle.

What is the age distribution of breast cancer cases?

You may be wondering around what age breast cancer develops and its progression throughout life. This slide shows a graphical representation of breast cancer cases by age group, and segmented by cancer stage.


Key facts:

  • Breast cancer occurs more in women older than age 40
  • Stage 1 and 2 cancers are the most common

Once you have breast cancer, does the tumor size play a role in estimated survival months?

One measure that is tracked during breast cancer treatment is tumor size. This slide shows a graphical representation of tumor size by estimated survival months. It is further segmented by cancer stage.


Key facts:

  • Tumor size does not correlate with the estimated months of survival
  • Generally, as cancer stages progresses (from T1 to T4), tumor size increases
  • Some T4 tumors can be smaller

Do any other factors like race or marital status correlate with survival likelihood?

Once you are diagnosed with breast cancer, you may wonder whether any factors apart from traits of the tumor correlate with your survival. This slide shows a graphical representation of either race or marital status by percentage of survival vs death.


Key facts:

  • Other races have higher rates of survival compared to white and black women
  • Married women have higher rates of survival compared to other marital statuses
